<p><p>The railroad ballast market is characterized by various types, each serving specific engineering needs. Granite ballast is favored for its durability and strength, making it ideal for high-traffic areas. Trap rock offers excellent interlocking properties and drainage capabilities. Quartzite is valued for its hardiness and resistance to weathering, while dolomite provides a cost-effective option with good load-bearing qualities. Additional types, classified as "Others," may include limestone or recycled materials, catering to specialized applications and sustainability goals in rail infrastructure.</p></p>
