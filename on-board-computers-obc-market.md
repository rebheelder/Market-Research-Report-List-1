<p><p>On-board Computers (OBC) are crucial for various applications in aerospace, automotive, and robotics, managing data and system operations in real-time. The market can be divided into two main types based on memory usage: volatile and non-volatile memory. Volatile memory, such as RAM, is temporary and requires power to maintain data, making it suitable for fast processing tasks. In contrast, non-volatile memory, like flash storage, retains information without power, essential for long-term data storage and system reliability.</p></p>
