<p><p>The cotton yarn market is categorized into carded yarn, combed yarn, and others. Carded yarn is made by carding cotton fibers to align them, producing a coarser, more affordable yarn suitable for everyday textiles. Combed yarn undergoes additional combing to remove short fibers and impurities, resulting in smoother, stronger, and higher-quality yarn used in premium fabrics. The "others" category includes specialized yarns like organic, blended, or compact yarns, catering to niche markets with unique properties or sustainability requirements.</p></p>
